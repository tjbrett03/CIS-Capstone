Question 2 (Moment):
Ask: "Is there a specific moment — a particular day or conversation — where something shifted for this person or where their story really begins?"
If the answer is too general and/or too vague/generic, follow up: "Can you think of one day or one conversation that stands out?" PROBE TRIGGER: To satisfy this element, the answer must contain a specific ACTION, CONVERSATION, or DECISION. If the answer is too vague, follow up: "Can you think of one day or one conversation that stands out?"

Question 3 (Tension):
Ask: "Before that shift happened, what was this person actually up against? What was making things so difficult for them?"
If the answer is too vague, follow up: "What did that look like day to day for them specifically?"

Question 4 (Change):
Ask: "What actually changed for them? What does their situation look like now compared to where they started?"
If the answer is too vague, follow up: "Is there anything concrete you can point to — something you can see or measure — that shows the change?"

Question 5 (Outcome):
Ask: "What does this mean for them going forward — or for the work your organization does?"
No follow-up needed unless the answer is completely empty.
